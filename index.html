Waterloo Fire Rescue is an emergency services department in northeastern Iowa. Established in 1904 as a professional organization of firefighters, the department continues to strive toward creating a safety conscious community in Waterloo with expanded roles in the emergency services. The department provides its citizens an advanced life support (ALS) ambulance service, performs fire suppression and rescue operations, conducts emergency management, and offers special services including hazardous materials mitigation and technical rescue. Waterloo Fire Rescue serves a resident population of roughly 70,000 people over an area of over 80 square miles. With just over 100 personnel, the department staffs six stations within city limits and uses three battalions. ...More
